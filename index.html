How did it all start?

My old Unity days..

Throughout my life, curiosity and learning have always been an essential part of my life, and possibly my well being. When I turned twelve and my dad got me my first computer, I nearly instantly dowloaded the game engine 'Unity 3D'. Why? Because of the simple reason that I wanted to learn how build computer games. Okey, insantly is not completely true, it did take some time to find Unity 3D. I downloaded tens of trash game engines before I purely out of luck from a youtube video stumbled over Unity 3D. It. Was. Amazing. At first I tried learning through Unity 3D's own tutorials, but it was either too hard to understand or too slow. So I began looking at youtube, which payed out sooo much. I watched tens, hundreds of tutorials and did my best to copy them. It went wondeful and I haven't had that much fun in a long time. I (not so carefully) started downloading models and textures from the very unsecure internet, which became my end. I managed to get some sort of virus on my computer that totally demolished it, and I asked my dad to fix it. He told me that he had to order disk's from the ASUS company to be able to restore the computer, because we couldn't even open it. Hmm, 2 weeks they said. I took half a year before I saw those disks. But at that point I had no excitment left, so when I finally after more than six months got my computer back I couldn't start again. I had no motivation. Once you loose motivation, oh boy is it hard to get it back. So I simply quit after having tried enjoying building games again numerus times. I started playing the new Counter-Strike: Global Offensive instead. This was in 2013 somewhere.
